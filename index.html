about me

Being from an Ecuadorian family has not only given me the gift of being bilingual but also the ability to experience two very distinct cultures. My dual identities have played a significant role in my life, teaching me lessons not many can experience. It has also driven me to want to break barriers in underrepresented roles, such as pursing a career in STEM. Along with many other reasons, STEM has always interested me, from conducting the classic vinegar and baking soda experiment at six years old to now being be a part of groups that revolve around STEM. Looking back to my elementary school years, I was constantly building and designing, whether it was a cardboard robot hand that could move with strings or starting a small business on Redbubble when I was ten. Combining both my passions, I became interested in biomedical engineering. As I grow throughout high school, I hope to find more opportunities to be involved in more STEM roles!
